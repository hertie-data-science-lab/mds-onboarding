## Is it necessary to learn maths for data science?

Modern libraries let you build models with very little maths - but a strong theoretical foundation is what lets you develop intuition, quickly grasp new methods, and understand fundamental research. Think of it as three layers:

<div class="math-pyramid">
  <div class="tier tier--1">
    <span class="tier__name">Libraries</span>
    <p class="tier__desc">Libraries, packages and frameworks that simplify the coding process.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="tier tier--2">
    <span class="tier__name">Source code</span>
    <p class="tier__desc">Transforming mathematical equations into code and abstracting the theory.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="tier tier--3">
    <span class="tier__name">Theories &amp; equations</span>
    <p class="tier__desc">Equations that model and approximate the problems of interest.</p>
  </div>
</div>

A strong theoretical foundation in mathematics helps you cultivate the intuition behind machine learning and deep learning models, and the ability to quickly grasp new methods, approaches and fundamental research.

## Two ways to approach maths

Everyone learns differently. Both of these approaches work - pick whichever suits you, or mix them.

<div class="two-approaches">
  <div class="approach">
    <div class="approach__tag">Theories then practice</div>
    <p><strong>Bottom-up:</strong> build a strong mathematical foundation and intuition before attempting complex concepts and projects.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="approach">
    <div class="approach__tag">Practice and theories</div>
    <p><strong>Top-down:</strong> tackle a project or concept head-on, reverse-engineer the concepts and code, and gradually build your mathematical intuition as you go.</p>
  </div>
</div>
